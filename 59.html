1. The effective energy of the Bremsstrahlung X-ray spectrum for tungsten is typically 80% of the maximum photon energy.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The effective energy of the Bremsstrahlung X-ray spectrum is the average energy, which is typically 50%–60% of the maximum. The peak energy is the most common photon energy.

2. In helical CT scanning, a pitch value greater than 1 results in a gap in data acquisition, reducing patient dose but potentially affecting image quality if not properly interpolated.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
A pitch of 1 means no overlap or separation of acquired data. A pitch >1 implies a gap in data acquisition, and <1 implies overlap. Pitch >1 reduces exposure time and dose, though most pitches above 1.5 are not clinically appropriate.

3. Modern X-ray equipment primarily utilizes three-phase, six-pulse generators to achieve a near-constant waveform with minimal ripple, similar to those used in the past.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Full-wave rectified and three-phase generators are no longer manufactured. Modern X-ray equipment uses high-frequency (HF) generators, converting mains AC to HF AC (1 kHz or higher), which can be converted to a steady high-voltage DC supply with no more than 1% ripple.

4. The intensity of an X-ray beam decreases across the field towards the anode edge due to the heel effect, as X-rays travelling in that direction cross more target material.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The heel effect causes the intensity of the beam to decrease across the field towards the anode edge (line A in Fig. 3.2), because X-rays travelling in that direction have more target material to cross and are thus attenuated more.

5. In Computed Radiography (CR) systems, the main limiting factor for spatial resolution is the size of the photostimulable phosphor crystals, which should be as large as possible for optimal image quality.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The main limiting factor for CR spatial resolution is the pixel size. Spatial resolution is improved with a thinner phosphor layer and smaller phosphor crystal size, as this decreases scatter.

6. The Mechanical Index (MI) in ultrasound provides an estimate of the likelihood of mechanical effects such as cavitation, with a threshold of 0.7 indicating potential for such effects.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The Mechanical Index (MI) estimates mechanical effects, defined as the peak rarefaction (negative) pressure divided by the square root of the ultrasound frequency. When MI reaches the threshold of 0.7, mechanical effects are thought to occur, and exam times should be reduced.

7. For optimal T1-weighted MRI images, a long Echo Time (TE) and a long Repetition Time (TR) are recommended.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
For maximum T1 contrast, a short TE (<30 ms) and a short TR (300-800 ms) are used. Long TE and TR are for T2-weighted images [134, 134t].

8. The use of a small compression paddle in mammography's spot views allows for increased tissue separation and higher resolution from decreased breast thickness.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The small field size, used for spot views with a small compression paddle, allows for increased tissue separation, distinction between true lesions and tissue overlap, and increased resolution from decreased thickness.

9. A thicker light guide in a conventional gamma camera detector head is optimal for maximizing intrinsic spatial resolution.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
To maximise the intrinsic resolution, one wishes to maximise the light detected by the nearest PMT, so a thin light guide is optimal. However, to optimise spatial linearity, a thicker light guide is best to spread the light signal between the maximum number of PMTs. This represents a fundamental trade-off in gamma camera design.

10. The mass number of an atom is determined by the combined number of protons and neutrons in its nucleus.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The nucleons (protons and neutrons) combine to yield the mass number of the atom.

11. The use of a wider photopeak acceptance window in PET imaging generally leads to improved image contrast due to better exclusion of scattered radiation.

Correct Answer: False
A wider photopeak window increases scatter coincidences, which reduces image contrast, although it does improve sensitivity. For optimal contrast, scatter correction is important before reconstruction.

12. The presence of a copper filter between two storage phosphor plates in single-exposure dual-energy radiography aims to filter lower keV X-rays for the rear plate, but results in lower SNR in the tissue-selective image due to fewer incident photons.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
In single-exposure dual-energy radiography, a copper filter separates the front and rear phosphor plates. The rear plate receives higher-energy photons, as the copper filters lower keV X-rays. Because there are fewer incident photons on the rear plate, this leads to a lower signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) in the tissue-selective image.

13. A higher acoustic impedance mismatch between two media leads to a greater fraction of sound energy being transmitted, facilitating better imaging across organ boundaries.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The greater the difference in acoustic impedance (Z) between two media, the greater the fraction of sound energy reflected (R), and thus less is transmitted. This is why total reflection occurs in air (negligible Z) and structures behind gas-filled organs cannot be imaged [154, 154t]. Small variations in soft tissues result in small fractions of reflection, enabling visualization of internal anatomical structures. [154, 154t]

14. In Diagnostic Reference Levels (DRLs) for CT, the Dose Length Product (DLP) is derived from the CT Dose Index (CTDI) multiplied by the scan length in centimeters.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The Dose Length Product (DLP), measured in mGy.cm, is defined as DLP = CTDIvol × L, where L is the total scan length. This makes DLP more useful for comparing doses to individual patients than CTDI alone.

15. The linear non-threshold (LNT) model, recommended by the ICRP, is universally accepted as it accurately reflects direct evidence of harm at low-level radiation exposures from atomic bomb survivor studies.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
While the LNT model is recommended by the ICRP, there is considerable disagreement about its applicability for low-dose exposures, as direct evidence of harm at these levels is limited and the atomic bomb data shows a threshold of 100-200 mSv for statistically significant risk increase, well above most medical exposures.

16. In CT imaging, structural noise is related to the processing of collected data, where high-resolution kernels can amplify noise due to their effect on high spatial frequencies.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Structural noise relates to the processing of the collected data. Individual reconstruction algorithms have different effects on the underlying data and its representation in the final image. For instance, high-resolution kernels amplify noise as it is a high frequency.

17. The primary reason a gamma camera's sensitivity remains approximately constant regardless of the distance between the source and the collimator face is that gamma photons increase in number with the square of the distance.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The sensitivity of a parallel-hole collimator remains approximately constant because the number of gamma photons passing through a particular collimator hole decreases with the square of the distance, but the number of holes through which the photons can pass increases as the square of the distance. These two effects cancel each other out.

18. A 'cold' lesion or photopenic defect in nuclear medicine images represents negative contrast, with its limit theoretically being -1.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Subject contrast (CS) is defined as (AL - AT) / AT, where AL and AT are activities in the lesion and healthy tissue, respectively. With this definition, a 'hot' lesion shows positive contrast whereas a 'cold' lesion, or photopenic defect, shows negative contrast. The limit of negative contrast is -1.

19. The use of a longer exposure time in X-ray production, while reducing the heat rating, generally leads to a decrease in geometric unsharpness.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Geometric unsharpness is reduced by decreasing focal spot size, decreasing object-detector distance, and increasing focus-detector distance. Movement unsharpness is reduced with shorter exposure times, immobilisation, and breath holding, but not geometric unsharpness. Longer exposure time reduces heat rating, but it increases movement unsharpness.

20. In SPECT imaging, the detector-to-patient distance should always be minimized to maximize the resolution obtained, a process known as 'body contouring'.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
In SPECT, the detector-to-patient distance should always be minimized to maximize the resolution obtained. This is known as 'body contouring' and is achieved by autocontouring devices.

21. The K-shell binding energy for molybdenum is 20 keV, making it an ideal filter material for mammography as it effectively removes X-rays above this energy.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Molybdenum has a K-edge of 20 keV, meaning it preferentially absorbs X-rays *above* this energy, and transmits those below. The filter is present to remove higher-energy X-ray photons, and trends towards monoenergetic beam production, thus the filter material (like Mo) attenuates X-rays with energy *above* its K-edge. The question implies it removes X-rays *above* 20 keV, which is correct in terms of filtering, but the goal is to transmit specific low-keV characteristic X-rays while removing higher-energy ones. The K-edge represents an abrupt increase in attenuation, meaning it becomes *more effective* at absorbing photons *just above* this energy, leading to a narrower spectrum.

22. In Computed Tomography, the automated tube current modulation can be impaired if only one scout image is used to determine the required mA for a patient, especially if the patient is not uniform in shape.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Photon starvation can occur if the mA required during modulation is beyond accepted tolerances, especially in very large patients. Patients are often not uniform in shape (e.g., anteroposterior dimension less than width), and mA modulation can be impaired if only one scout image is used to determine the required mA.

23. For diagnostic purposes, all radiopharmaceuticals used in nuclear medicine, including those for 99Tcm, must be sterilized by terminal heat treatment prior to administration.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Radiopharmaceuticals for injection must be produced in a sterile, pyrogen-free environment, but due to their short shelf life (e.g., 99Tcm), preparations often have to be released prior to the results of sterility testing being known. They are prepared aseptically rather than by terminal heat treatment (which might degrade the pharmaceutical). The statement says *must be sterilized by terminal heat treatment*, which is not always the case given their short shelf lives and specific preparation methods.

24. The fundamental problem with a PET/MRI system, leading to the development of digital PET/CT systems, is that Photomultiplier Tubes (PMTs) in PET detectors do not function correctly in large magnetic fields.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The fundamental problem with a PET/MRI system is that PMTs in a PET detector do not function correctly in the presence of large magnetic fields. This issue drove the development of solid-state light detection solutions, which in turn led to digital PET/CT systems.

25. In the image intensifier of a fluoroscopy system, the output screen is typically composed of caesium iodide crystals with sodium to convert X-ray photons to light photons.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The input screen converts X-ray photons to light photons, using caesium iodide crystals with sodium. The output screen converts electrons to light, using zinc cadmium sulphide doped with silver (ZnCdS:Ag).

26. For a given element and shell level, each electron binding energy is fixed, and when an inner shell electron returns to its original shell after excitation, the excess energy is re-emitted as a photon, yielding characteristic X-rays.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Each binding energy is fixed for a given element and shell level. When an inner shell electron is excited through the external deposition of energy, it can be raised to a higher electron shell. When the electron returns to its original shell, the excess energy is re-emitted as a photon. The energy value of the photon is consistent for each element, yielding characteristic photons (termed X-rays).

27. The main role of the Medical Physics Expert (MPE) under IR(ME)R 2017 is to perform the justification of all individual medical exposures, ensuring patient benefit outweighs risk.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The **practitioner** is required to justify all medical exposures. The MPE's role is to provide scientific advice, clarify the role, and assist with risk and hazard assessment, and contribute to optimization, but not to perform the justification of individual medical exposures.

28. The conversion factors for calculating effective doses from Dose-Area Product (DAP) or Dose Length Product (DLP) are generally highest for radiosensitive organs in the abdomen and chest.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The conversion factors for calculation of effective doses are generally highest for the more radiosensitive organs in the abdomen and chest and lowest for the head and limbs.

29. For the purpose of dose limits under IRR 2017, trainees under 18 years of age are subject to the same effective dose limits as other persons (1 mSv per year).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Trainees under 18 have a whole-body effective dose limit of 6 mSv per year. Other persons have a limit of 1 mSv per year. Employees over 18 have a limit of 20 mSv per year.

30. The optimal working life of a 99Tcm generator is around seven to ten days, after which the activity extracted is unlikely to be sufficient for a full day’s activity in a busy nuclear medicine department.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
The optimal working life of a 99Tcm generator is around seven to ten days. Although some activity can be extracted beyond this time, it is unlikely to be sufficient for a full day’s activity in a busy nuclear medicine department.

31. Increasing the kV in X-ray production primarily increases the quantity of photons, without significantly affecting the maximum or effective energy of the spectrum.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Increased kV shifts the spectrum upwards and to the right, leading to increased maximum and effective energy, and an increased total number of photons. Increased mA, on the other hand, increases *only* the quantity of photons.

32. In MRI, B1 inhomogeneities, also known as dielectric artefacts, are more problematic at higher field strengths, especially when imaging parts of the body with significant fluid content.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Dielectric artefacts arise when the wavelength of the RF pulse is similar to the structure being imaged. This can be problematic at 3 T when imaging abdomens, particularly when there is lots of fluid present, making it advisable to scan pregnant patients and patients with ascites at 1.5 T.

33. The range of a secondary electron is directly proportional to the density of the material it travels through.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The range of a secondary electron is the distance it can travel before losing its initial excitation energy and is inversely proportional to the density of the material.

34. In Computed Tomography, cupping artifact can result in CT numbers being lower in the center of the patient than they should be, and is partially correctable by beam-hardening algorithms or bow-tie filters.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Cupping occurs because the center of the patient is invariably crossed by hardened beams, whereas tissues nearer the surface are crossed by less filtered beams for part of the rotation. This results in CT numbers being lower in the center. It can be corrected by beam-hardening algorithms or reduced with bow-tie filters.

35. The DQE (Detective Quantum Efficiency) of a Computed Radiography (CR) system is typically around 65%, which is superior to Digital Radiography (DR) systems.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
CR systems can achieve a DQE of ~30%, while DR systems can achieve ~65%. Therefore, DR systems have superior DQE.

36. In ultrasound, a high Q transducer produces a pure tone and is typically used in Continuous Wave (CW) Doppler applications.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
A high Q transducer produces a pure tone and only responds to a single frequency, as in CW. A low Q transducer has a short ring-down time, produces short pulses, and produces and responds to a wide range of frequencies, as in PW.

37. Radiologists are directly responsible for the day-to-day MRI safety by ensuring compliance with the MRI local rules, according to MHRA guidelines.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
The **MR Responsible Person** is responsible for day-to-day MRI safety. Radiologists play a key role in MRI safety by understanding risks to make informed choices on scan appropriateness, but they are not typically the MR Responsible Person.

38. Pulsed fluoroscopy, by supplying X-rays in pulses rather than continuously, is primarily used to reduce patient dose rate in angiographic imaging.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Pulsed fluoroscopy is used to reduce dose rate in angiographic imaging. The X-ray generator is switched on and off to supply pulses of X-rays, which helps minimize patient dose.

39. In nuclear medicine, Parametric Images typically display the behaviour of the radiopharmaceutical averaged within the entire Region of Interest (ROI).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Time Activity Curves (TACs) demonstrate the behaviour of the radiopharmaceutical averaged within the entire ROI. Parametric images are generated by deriving a parameter from TACs for *each pixel* and displaying this in an image form, replacing the counts in a pixel by this parameter.

40. The DICOM standard ensures consistent perceptible shades of grey across different medical display devices through the use of a lookup table (LUT) called the Greyscale Standard Display Function (GSDF).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) ensures consistent perceptible shades of grey across devices via the DICOM greyscale standard display function (GSDF), which maps digital driving levels to consistent luminance output.
