Farr's Physics for Medical Imaging: Chapter 1 Quiz

Test your knowledge on the fundamental principles of medical imaging physics from Chapter 1 of the 3rd edition.

1. Ionizing radiation, as described in medical imaging, exclusively involves X-rays.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Ionizing radiations involve both **X-rays** and **gamma rays**, while non-ionizing radiations involve radio-frequency and sound.

2. The nucleus contributes the majority of the mass of an atom.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: Each atom is defined by its central nucleus, which contributes the **majority of the mass**.

3. Radioisotopes are defined as stable atoms with the same atomic number but a different number of neutrons.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: **Isotopes** have the same atomic number but a different number of neutrons; some of these isotopes are **unstable** and decay, which are called **radioisotopes**.

4. K-shell electrons in an atom have less binding energy compared to L-shell electrons for a given element.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Each shell has different binding energies, with **EK > EL > EM**, meaning K-shell electrons have **higher** binding energy than L-shell electrons.

5. All forms of electromagnetic (EM) radiation travel at the same velocity as light when in a vacuum.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: All forms of electromagnetic (EM) radiation travel at the **same velocity as light when in vacuo** (2.98 × 10⁸ ms⁻¹).

6. Photon energy is directly proportional to its wavelength.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Photon energy (E) is **inversely proportional** to wavelength (λ), as shown by the formula E = hc/λ.

7. In X-ray tube target interactions, approximately 99% of the electron's kinetic energy is converted into X-rays.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Approximately **99%** of the electron's kinetic energy is converted to **heat**, while only about **1%** is converted to X-rays.

8. Increasing the kilovoltage (kV) in an X-ray tube only increases the quantity of photons produced, without affecting their energy.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Increased kV **shifts the X-ray spectrum upwards and to the right**, leading to increased maximum and effective energy, as well as an increased total number of photons.

9. Bremsstrahlung radiation accounts for approximately 80% of produced X-rays from target interactions.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: **Bremsstrahlung** (braking radiation) accounts for **80% of produced X-rays**, resulting from bombarding electrons being deflected by the nucleus.

10. Filtration in X-ray imaging primarily removes high-energy photons before they reach the patient.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Filtration involves the removal of **lower-energy photons** before they reach the patient, as these mostly contribute to the dose with little impact on image formation.

11. The Half-Value Layer (HVL) is defined as the thickness of a given material that will halve the intensity of a wide beam of X-radiation.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: The HVL is the thickness of a given material that will halve the intensity of a ***narrow*** beam of X-radiation.

12. The probability of photoelectric absorption increases with increased incident photon energy.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: The probability of photoelectric absorption **decreases** with increased incident photon energy (1/E³) and increases with the increased atomic number of the material (Z³).

13. Fluorescence is characterized by a delayed emission of visible photons, facilitated by electron traps.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Fluorescence involves the emission of photons nearly **instantaneously** (10⁻¹⁰ to 10⁻⁷ s), whereas **phosphorescence** involves delayed emission facilitated by electron traps.

14. In beta decay, a neutron is converted into a proton and a positron.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: In **beta decay** (beta minus decay), a neutron is converted into a proton and an **electron** (beta particle), which is emitted from the nucleus. The conversion of a proton to a neutron and a **positron** is positron emission (beta plus decay).

15. The physical half-life of a radionuclide, when used in the body, is shortened by the physiological clearance of the pharmaceutical compound.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: The **physical half-life** is an **immutable property** of the radionuclide. Physiological clearance affects the ***effective*** half-life, which is shorter than both the physical and biological half-lives as both occur simultaneously.

16. Technetium-99m (99Tcm) is considered ideally suited for imaging because its pure gamma emission contributes entirely to usable image formation without particulate emissions.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: 99Tcm decays by **isomeric transition** with **pure gamma emission** (140 keV gamma rays), making it ideally suited as all emitted radiation contributes to the image, unlike particulate emissions.

17. The maximum activity of Technetium-99m (99Tcm) available for extraction from a 99Mo/99Tcm generator is achieved approximately 24 hours after the last elution.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True
Explanation: By a "happy coincidence," the half-lives of parent 99Mo (67.2 hours) and daughter 99Tcm (6 hours) mean that the **maximum activity of 99Tcm is achieved approximately 24 hours after the last elution**.

18. Compartmental leakage is a radiopharmaceutical localization method where molecules are physically trapped within small blood vessels by their size.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: This describes **capillary trapping**, where particles are physically trapped in small blood vessels due to their size. **Compartmental leakage** identifies abnormal openings in otherwise closed compartments, such as gastrointestinal bleeding detection using 99Tcm labeled red blood cells.

19. Practitioners and researchers should always rely solely on the information, methods, compounds, or experiments described in this book without independent verification.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: Practitioners and researchers must always **rely on their own experience and knowledge**, and due to rapid advances in medical sciences, **independent verification** of diagnoses and drug dosages should be made.

20. The outermost electron shell, known as the valence shell, can hold up to 32 electrons for elements like Sodium (Na).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False
Explanation: While the N shell can hold 32 electrons, the **valence shell** (outermost) can **only ever have eight electrons** within it to achieve a complete outer shell octet.
