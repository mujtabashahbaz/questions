Back Pain Assessment Form

1. Onset and Duration


Today (Started within last 24 hours)
Within last week (1–7 days ago)
1–4 weeks ago (Started recently, more than a week ago)
More than 1 month ago (Chronic pain for over a month)

Sudden (Came all of a sudden)
Gradual (Slowly developed over time)
After lifting/strain (Started after lifting something heavy or bending)
After a fall/accident (Pain started after injury or accident)

2. Pain Characteristics


Stops at thigh (Pain goes down but stops around upper leg)
Goes below knee (Pain goes past knee, towards calf or foot)
To both legs (Pain radiates to both sides)
Only one leg (Pain goes down only left or right leg)

Sharp (Like a stabbing or knife-like pain)
Shooting (Sudden electric-shock type pain)
Burning (Feels hot or burning sensation)
Dull/Achy (Constant dull pain or heaviness)

3. Aggravating & Relieving Factors


Walking (Pain increases when walking)
Sitting (Pain worse while sitting for long)
Standing (Pain worse when standing)
Bending forward (Pain worsens on bending forward)
Coughing/Sneezing (Pain shoots when coughing or sneezing)

Rest (Pain reduces when resting)
Lying down (Pain improves when lying flat)
Bending forward (Pain reduces when leaning forward)
Sitting (Pain less when sitting)

4. Associated Symptoms

Numbness (Legs or feet feel numb)
Tingling (Pins and needles feeling)
Weakness (Legs feel weak or hard to move)
Bladder/Bowel issues (Trouble controlling urine or stool)
