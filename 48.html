FRCR Part 1 Radiology Physics Quiz

Test your understanding of Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) principles from Farr's Physics for Medical Imaging, 3rd Edition (Chapter 6).

1. During a spontaneous quench in a superconducting MRI scanner, the liquid helium undergoes a phase transition to liquid nitrogen and is safely released into the scan room.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

During a quench, liquid helium turns into a **gas** and is vented out of the scanner via the quench pipe, as it is extremely cold and can **displace oxygen**, making it potentially dangerous. It does not turn into liquid nitrogen.

2. MR Conditional items can be safely used in any MR Environment without the need for specific conditions or guidelines.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

MR Conditional items have demonstrated safety in the MR Environment, but **only within defined conditions** that address the static magnetic field, switched gradient magnetic field, and radio-frequency (RF) fields, potentially with specific item configurations.

3. Time-varying magnetic fields generated by gradient coils in MRI scanners primarily contribute to acoustic noise, but do not cause heating or vibration of implanted devices.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Time-varying magnetic fields cause acoustic noise due to forces on the gradient coils, but they also lead to **heating and vibration** of implanted devices, primarily due to Lenz's law.

4. According to the MHRA guidelines, a 1.0°C whole-body temperature rise is the maximum permissible in the 'Normal Operating Mode' for MRI scanners.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

For the 'Normal Operating Mode', the maximum whole-body temperature rise is **0.5°C**. A 1.0°C rise is permitted in the 'First Level (Controlled)' mode. [127, Table 6.1]

5. Macrocyclic gadolinium-based contrast agents are generally associated with a higher risk of nephrogenic systemic fibrosis (NSF) compared to linear agents due to their more stable chemical structure.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Macrocyclic contrast agents are considered to carry a **lower risk** of NSF because they are chemically **more stable** than linear ones.

6. In clinical MRI, hydrogen nuclei are exclusively used for imaging because they possess an even number of protons, which inherently exhibits nuclear magnetic resonance.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

While hydrogen is predominantly used, any nuclei with an **odd number of protons** will exhibit nuclear MR. Hydrogen has a single proton (odd number).

7. The Larmor frequency of hydrogen protons in an MRI scanner is directly proportional to both the gyromagnetic ratio of the element and the strength of the applied static magnetic field (B0).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True

The Larmor frequency (u0) is defined by the equation u0 = gB0, where 'g' is the gyromagnetic ratio and 'B0' is the static magnetic field strength, indicating a direct proportionality.

8. Immediately after a 90-degree RF excitation pulse, the longitudinal magnetization (Mz) instantly recovers to its initial equilibrium value (M0), while the transverse magnetization (Mxy) remains at zero.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Immediately after a 90-degree RF pulse, Mz (longitudinal magnetization) is tipped into the x-y plane and becomes **zero**, while Mxy (transverse magnetization) reaches its maximum value of M0. Mz then recovers back to M0 over the T1 relaxation time.

9. In slice selection, the thickness of the excited slice is primarily determined by the gyromagnetic ratio and is independent of the applied gradient strength.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

The thickness of the excited slice depends on the applied **gradient strength (GSS)**, the gyromagnetic ratio, and the transmitter bandwidth (range of frequencies) of the RF pulse.

10. Aliasing artifacts, characterized by tissues outside the field of view appearing wrapped into the image, are a common problem in frequency encoding due to insufficient sampling during readout.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Aliasing (or wrap) artifacts occur in the **phase-encoding direction** when tissues outside the FOV cannot be distinguished due to phase shifts beyond 360 degrees.

11. Tissues with shorter T1 relaxation times, such as cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), appear bright on T1-weighted (T1w) images because they quickly recover their longitudinal magnetization.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

CSF has a **long T1 relaxation time** and therefore recovers slowly, appearing with **low signal (dark)** on T1w images. Tissues with short T1 (e.g., fat) recover quickly and appear bright.

12. In a basic Spin Echo (SE) sequence, the 180-degree refocusing pulse completely eliminates all dephasing effects, including those caused by intrinsic spin-spin interactions (T2) and magnetic susceptibility differences (T2*).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

The 180-degree refocusing pulse in SE sequences rephases the T2* dephasing caused by magnetic field inhomogeneities (including susceptibility differences), but it **does not rephase the random, irreversible dephasing due to intrinsic spin-spin (T2) relaxation**. Thus, only the random T2 relaxation affects the contrast in the final image.

13. Fast Spin Echo (FSE) sequences, while reducing scan time, typically result in lower Specific Absorption Rate (SAR) values compared to basic Spin Echo sequences due to the rapid acquisition of multiple echoes.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

FSE/TSE sequences have **high SAR** values. The use of multiple large RF pulses in a short space of time (to generate multiple echoes per TR) leads to increased energy deposition.

14. Gradient Echo (GRE) sequences are generally faster than Spin Echo (SE) sequences because they employ a 180-degree refocusing pulse, which allows for shorter repetition times.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

GRE sequences are faster because they **do not use a 180-degree refocusing pulse**. Instead, they use gradients to dephase and rephase the signal. The absence of this pulse also means not all longitudinal magnetization is tipped into the transverse plane, allowing for shorter TRs.

15. Fat saturation techniques are generally more effective at lower field strengths (e.g., 1.5 T) because the chemical shift between fat and water resonant frequencies is greater, allowing for better spectral separation.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Fat saturation is **easier to achieve at higher field strengths** because there is a **greater separation** (chemical shift) between the fat and water peaks (e.g., 440 Hz at 3 T vs 220 Hz at 1.5 T).

16. In Dixon imaging, a water-suppressed image is created by adding the in-phase (IP) and out-of-phase (OP) images, which causes the fat signals to cancel out while water signals add together.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

A water-suppressed image (i.e., a fat-only image) is created by **subtracting** the out-of-phase (OP) image from the in-phase (IP) image (Sfat = 0.5(SIP - SOP) or Sfat = 0.5(SOP - SIP) depending on phase relationship), which causes **water signals to cancel out** and fat signals to add. Adding the IP and OP images results in a water-only image (Swater = 0.5(SOP + SIP)).

17. The center of k-space in MRI primarily contains information related to the image's spatial resolution, whereas the outer edges contribute more to the overall image contrast.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

The **center of k-space** contains the image **contrast information**, while the **edge of k-space** contains the **resolution information**.

18. Parallel imaging techniques, such as SENSE or GRAPPA, reduce scan time by decreasing the number of phase-encoding steps acquired, which consequently leads to a reduction in the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR).

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: True

Parallel imaging reduces scan time by acquiring fewer phase-encoding steps. This, however, leads to a reduction in SNR. For example, reducing scan time by 50% can reduce SNR to approximately 70%.

19. Susceptibility artifacts, frequently observed near metal implants, are more prominent on Spin Echo (SE) sequences compared to Gradient Echo (GRE) sequences because SE sequences lack a 180-degree refocusing pulse.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

Susceptibility artifacts are **less prominent on SE sequences** because the **180-degree refocusing pulse helps to rephase** the dephasing caused by local magnetic-field distortions. GRE sequences, which lack this pulse, are therefore more susceptible to these artifacts.

20. Functional MRI (fMRI) studies typically rely on the Blood Oxygenation Level-Dependent (BOLD) signal, which increases with higher static magnetic field strengths (B0) primarily due to the diamagnetic properties of oxyhaemoglobin.

Show Explanation and Correct Answer

Correct Answer: False

The BOLD signal increases with B0. However, oxyhaemoglobin is **diamagnetic** (weakly opposes B0), while deoxyhaemoglobin is paramagnetic (weakly enhances B0). The BOLD signal primarily reflects changes in the concentration of paramagnetic deoxyhaemoglobin, not the diamagnetic properties of oxyhaemoglobin.
